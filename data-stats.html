Lesson 01 · Data & AI

Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics summarise a dataset with a few numbers. Instead of inspecting all 10,000 rows, you can describe your data with mean, median, standard deviation, and quartiles.

📊 Central Tendency
  • Mean: Sum of values ÷ count. Sensitive to outliers.
  • Median: Middle value when sorted. Robust to outliers.
  • Mode: Most frequent value. For categorical data.
📐 Spread
  • Range: Max − Min
  • Variance: Average squared deviation from mean
  • Standard Deviation (σ): √Variance — same unit as data
  • IQR: Q3 − Q1 — middle 50% of data

Distributions

A distribution shows how values are spread. The normal distribution (bell curve) appears everywhere in nature and is the foundation of most statistical methods.

🔔 The 68-95-99.7 Rule (Normal Distribution)
  • 68% of data falls within 1 standard deviation of the mean (μ ± σ)
  • 95% falls within 2 standard deviations (μ ± 2σ)
  • 99.7% falls within 3 standard deviations (μ ± 3σ)

🔔 Normal Distribution Visualizer

⚠️ Skewness

Not all distributions are normal. Right-skewed: long tail to the right (income, house prices — a few millionaires pull the mean up). Left-skewed: long tail to the left. Use median instead of mean for skewed data.

Challenge 2+15 XP

Outlier Detection

In a normal distribution with μ=100 and σ=10, a value of 135 is how many standard deviations from the mean?

z = (value − μ) / σ = (135 − 100) / 10 = 35 / 10 = 3.5
Lesson 03 · Data & AI

Correlation

Correlation measures how two variables move together. The Pearson correlation coefficient r ranges from −1 to +1. It's the backbone of feature selection in machine learning.

📈 Interpreting r
  • r = 1.0: Perfect positive correlation (height and weight)
  • r = 0.7: Strong positive correlation
  • r = 0.0: No linear correlation
  • r = −0.7: Strong negative correlation
  • r = −1.0: Perfect negative correlation
⚠️ Correlation ≠ Causation

Ice cream sales and drowning rates are correlated — both increase in summer. But ice cream doesn't cause drowning; a confounding variable (hot weather) causes both. Always look for confounders before claiming causation.

r = Σ[(x − μₓ)(y − μᵧ)] / (n · σₓ · σᵧ)

Probability Basics

Probability quantifies uncertainty. It's the language of machine learning — every prediction is a probability. Understanding basic probability is essential for understanding how models work.

🎲 Probability Rules
  • P(A) ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain)
  • P(A) + P(not A) = 1
  • P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B)
  • Independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)
  • Conditional: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
🔮 Bayes' Theorem

P(A|B) = P(B|A) × P(A) / P(B)

Used in spam filters (probability email is spam given it contains "FREE"), medical diagnosis, and naive Bayes classifiers. It lets you update beliefs with new evidence.
