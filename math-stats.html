Lesson 01 · Statistics & Probability

Measures of Center

Before you can understand data, you need to summarize it. The three core measures of center — mean, median, and mode — each tell a different story about where your data clusters.

MeasureHow to CalculateBest forWeakness
Mean (average)Sum all values ÷ countSymmetric dataSensitive to outliers
MedianMiddle value when sortedSkewed data, incomeIgnores extreme values
ModeMost frequent valueCategorical dataMay not exist or be unique
💡 Which to Use?

The mean salary at a company is $90,000 but the CEO earns $5M — that single outlier pulls the mean up massively. The median salary (the middle one) better represents the typical employee. For house prices, clothing sizes, most frequent purchases — think about which measure actually answers your question.

Lesson 02 · Statistics & Probability

Spread & Variance

Knowing where data centers is only half the story. How spread out is it? Two datasets can have the same mean but completely different shapes — variance and standard deviation capture the spread.

📐 Key Formulas
  • Range = max − min (simple, but sensitive to outliers)
  • Variance (σ²) = average of squared differences from mean
  • Standard Deviation (σ) = √variance — same units as data
σ = √[ Σ(xᵢ − μ)² / N ]
TermSymbolMeaning
Population meanμ (mu)Average of entire population
Sample meanx̄ (x-bar)Average of a sample
Population varianceσ²Average squared deviation (divide by N)
Sample variances²Unbiased estimate (divide by N−1)
Standard deviationσ or sSquare root of variance
IQRQ3 − Q1Range of middle 50% — robust to outliers
🤖 Why This Matters in ML

Feature normalization (StandardScaler) subtracts the mean and divides by standard deviation. Every neural network training pipeline starts with this. If your data has σ=1000 and another feature has σ=0.01, gradient descent will be completely unbalanced.

Probability Basics

Probability measures how likely an event is to occur. It ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). Every random algorithm, machine learning model, and A/B test is built on this foundation.

🎲 Core Definitions
  • Sample space (S): all possible outcomes
  • Event (E): a subset of the sample space
  • P(E) = |favorable outcomes| / |total outcomes|
  • Complement P(Eᶜ) = 1 − P(E)
RuleFormulaWhen to use
Addition (OR)P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B)A or B occurs
Multiplication (AND)P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B) [if independent]Both A and B occur
ComplementP(Aᶜ) = 1 − P(A)A does not occur

Lesson 04 · Statistics & Probability

Conditional Probability & Bayes' Theorem

Conditional probability is the probability of event A given that event B has already occurred. It updates your beliefs when you have new information — and it's the engine behind spam filters, medical tests, and recommendation systems.

P(A | B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B)
🔬 Bayes' Theorem

Bayes' theorem reverses the conditioning: given P(B|A), find P(A|B).

P(A|B) = [ P(B|A) × P(A) ] / P(B)

Prior P(A) = initial belief. Likelihood P(B|A) = evidence. Posterior P(A|B) = updated belief.

📧 Real Example: Spam Filter

P(spam) = 0.2 (prior). P("free"|spam) = 0.7. P("free"|not spam) = 0.05. If an email contains "free", what's P(spam|"free")? Bayes gives us: P(spam|"free") ≈ 0.78 — the word "free" strongly updates our belief toward spam.

# Bayes' Theorem in Python p_spam = 0.2 p_nospam = 0.8 p_free_given_spam = 0.7 p_free_given_nospam = 0.05 # P("free") = P(free|spam)×P(spam) + P(free|¬spam)×P(¬spam) p_free = p_free_given_spam * p_spam + p_free_given_nospam * p_nospam # Bayes: P(spam|"free") p_spam_given_free = (p_free_given_spam * p_spam) / p_free print(round(p_spam_given_free, 3)) # 0.778

Probability Distributions

A probability distribution tells you how likely each outcome is. Different phenomena follow different distributions — and recognizing which one your data follows is a superpower in data analysis and ML.

DistributionShapeWhen to useExample
UniformFlat — all equalAll outcomes equally likelyFair die rolls
Normal (Gaussian)Bell curveNatural variation, errorsHeights, IQ scores, noise
BinomialDiscrete, bell-ishn independent yes/no trialsNumber of heads in 10 flips
PoissonRight-skewedCount of rare eventsServer requests/second
ExponentialDecayingTime between eventsTime between arrivals

📏 The 68-95-99.7 Rule

For a normal distribution: 68% of data falls within 1σ of the mean, 95% within 2σ, and 99.7% within 3σ. If test scores are normally distributed with μ=70 and σ=10, then 95% of students scored between 50 and 90.

🤖
Machine Learning
Gaussian Naive Bayes assumes features are normally distributed. Most activation functions were chosen based on distribution theory.
🧪
A/B Testing
Statistical significance in A/B tests uses the normal distribution. p-values tell you if a result is likely by chance.
⚡
Server Monitoring
Request rates follow Poisson. Response times follow exponential. Knowing this lets you set better alerts and capacity plans.
📈
Finance
Stock returns are (approximately) normally distributed. Portfolio risk models are built entirely on variance and correlation.
